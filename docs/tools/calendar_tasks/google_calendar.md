# Google Calendar

## What it is
Google Calendar is a time-management and scheduling calendar service developed by Google.

## What problem it solves
It provides a robust, ubiquitous, and reliable platform for managing personal and family schedules, accessible from any device.

## Where it fits in the pipeline
**Decide / Sync**

## Typical use cases (in this homelab / family automation context)
- **Primary Schedule**: Managing work and personal appointments.
- **Family Sync**: Sharing calendars with family members to coordinate school runs and activities.
- **Automation Destination**: Receiving events automatically from AI extraction pipelines (e.g., from an email about a school activity).

## Integration points
- **n8n / Zapier / Make**: Native nodes for reading and writing events.
- **Home Assistant**: Triggering home events based on calendar start/end times.
- **CalDAV**: Can be accessed via CalDAV for integration with self-hosted clients.

## Licensing and cost
- **Open Source**: No
- **Cost**: Free (with optional paid workspace tiers)
- **Free tier**: Yes
- **Self-hostable**: No

## Strengths
- Unmatched reliability and cross-device support.
- Deep integration with other Google services (Gmail, Meet).
- Excellent mobile apps.

## Limitations
- Privacy concerns associated with cloud-hosted data.
- Dependence on Google's ecosystem.
